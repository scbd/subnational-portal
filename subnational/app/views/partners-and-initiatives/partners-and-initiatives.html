Global Partnership

The Global Partnership on Local and Subnational Action for Biodiversity was established in 2008 with the goal of bringing together all the relevant networks and initiatives involved, directly or indirectly, in implementing the Convention on Biological Diversity. Membership is open to all. Within the Global Partnership, advisory committees of cities and of subnational governments provide specific direction based on hands-on experience, while a group of urban biodiversity researchers, URBIO, add scientific expertise to the Partnership. Advisory Committee of Cities
The Advisory Committee of Cities to the Global Partnership on Local and Subnational Action for Biodiversity consists of representatives of cities that have hosted recent meetings of the biennial Conference of the Parties to the CBD (Hyderabad, Nagoya, Bonn, Curitiba), as well as the host city of the Secretariat, Montreal, and other cities that have been particularly active in the area of subnational implementation (Montpellier, Mexico City). The island-state Singapore has, in addition, played a continued supporting role to the Advisory Committee. The Advisory Committee of Cities has shown initiative not only in its advisory capacity but also through example: led by Mexico City, an exhibition of urban biodiversity in all the cities of the Committee was compiled and displayed at CBD COP 11 in Hyderabad, and at local venues. In addition Curitiba, Bonn and Nagoya have hosted meetings of the Global Partnership.

Advisory Committee of Subnational Governments
The Advisory Committee of Subnational Governments to the Global Partnership on Local and Subnational Action for Biodiversity consists of representatives of subnational governments that have hosted recent meetings of the biennial Conference of the Parties to the CBD (Gangwon Province, Andhra Pradesh State, Aichi Prefecture, Paraná State), as well as the host subnational government of the Secretariat, Quebec, and other subnational governments that have been particularly active in the area of subnational implementation (Catalonia, São Paulo State). The Advisory Committee of Subnational Governments has shown initiative not only in its advisory capacity but also through example: led by Catalonia and Quebec, an action plan of projects is being compiled and implemented, creating a model for other subnational governments to follow. In addition Catalonia, Gangwon Province and Paraná State have hosted meetings of the Global Partnership, and Quebec is planning to host the next meeting of the Advisory Committee of Subnational Governments in 2015.

URBIO
The group “Urban Biodiversity and Design” (URBIO) consists of researchers with a focus on urban biodiversity, affiliated with the Global Partnership on Local and Subnational Action for Biodiversity. URBIO have convened conferences on urban biodiversity research and design in parallel with the past three and upcoming biennial Conference of the Parties to the CBD, the outcomes of which have been fed into summits on cities and subnational governments for biodiversity, also in parallel with the COP. URBIO are currently compiling a research agenda, with broad input, indicating which areas of urban biodiversity research are most urgent and necessary. Click here to visit URBIO’s website.

ICLEI

ICLEI-Local Governments for Sustainability is a powerful movement of 12 mega-cities, 100 super-cities and urban regions, 450 large cities as well as 450 medium-sized cities and towns in 86 countries. The organization promotes local action for global sustainability and support cities to become sustainable, resilient, resource-efficient, biodiverse, low-carbon; to build a smart infrastructure; and to develop an inclusive, green urban economy with the ultimate aim to achieve healthy and happy communities. Click here to visit ICLEI’s website.

ICLEI’s Cities Biodiversity Centre, located in Cape Town, South Africa, is responsible for ICLEI’s global work on biodiversity. Since 2011, the Cities Biodiversity Centre has seconded an expert to the Secretariat of the CBD. This unique and unprecedented contribution has been a major driving force behind the CBD’s support of subnational implementation.

Local Action for Biodiversity (LAB) is the flagship programme that made ICLEI’s Cities Biodiversity Centre famous. Practical, hands-on and in line with the objectives of the CBD, LAB is beginning to expand into regional initiatives, the latest of which is South Korea. Click here to learn more about LAB.

MediverCities

MediverCities is an open network of Mediterranean local governments, their associations and partners – national/subnational governments, academia, scientific institutions, internationals organizations – that aim to improve the protection and management of biological resources and ecosystem services in the Mediterranean Basin. For more information, click here to visit MediverCities website.

MiTin

The Maritime Innovative Territories International Network is a network of regional public authorities, scientific institutions and economic agencies with the aim of developing sustainable economic growth for the new “blue economy”. For more information, click here to visit MiTin’s website.

nrg4SD

The Network of Regional Governments for Sustainable Development (nrg4SD) is a non-profit international organisation representing subnational governments and associations of subnational governments at global level. Altogether, nrg4SD represents around 600 territories in the World. The Network focuses on 3 main thematic areas – climate change, biodiversity, and water resources and sanitation – and the cross-cutting theme of sustainable development. For more information, click here to visit nrg4SD’s website.

Stockholm Resilience Center

The Stockholm Resilience Centre advances research on the governance of social-ecological systems with a special emphasis on resilience - the ability to deal with change and continue to develop. For more information, click here to visit the Stockholm Resilience Center’s website.

Nature of Cities Blog

The Nature of Cities is a virtual magazine and discussion site on cities as ecosystems. It is a global collective of contributors, an essay and discussion site devoted to cities as social-ecological spaces, ecosystems of people, buildings, open spaces, and nature. City design with nature at the center is key to urban resilience, sustainability, and livability. For more information, click here to visit the Nature of Cities Blog.

United Nations - Habitat

UN-HABITAT is an active member of the CBD Global Partnership on Local and Subnational Action for Biodiversity since 2007. UN-HABITAT and the CBD Secretariat have worked together to develop the Supporting Local Action for Biodiversity: the Role of National Governments publication to provide biodiversity decision-makers at the national level with practical information and advice on how to support and encourage biodiversity protection and management at the local level. For more information, click here to visit UN-Habitat’s website.

United Nations Economic Social and Cultural Organization

The United Nations Economic Social and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) began in 1949 with the firm belief that peace must be establish on the basis of humanity’s moral and intellectual solidarity. Today, UNESCO’s message has advocates holistic policies that are capable of addressing the social, environmental and economic dimensions of sustainable development. At COP 10 in Nagoya, Japan UNESCO partnered with the CBD to strengthen the links between cultural and biological diversity initiatives for the benefit of present and future generations. For more information, click here to view the UNESCO website.

United Nations Environment Programme

The United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) acts as a catalyst, advocate, educator and facilitator to promote the wise use and sustainable development of the global environment. Working with regional Secretariats, like the CBD, UNEP assesses environmental trends, develops instruments and strengthens institutions on a global, national and regional level to promote wise management of the environment. For more information, click here to view the UNEP website.

Food and Agriculture Organization

The Food and Agricultural Organization’s (FAO) efforts aim to make sure people have regular access to enough high-quality food to lead active, healthy lives. The three main goals are: the eradication of hunger, food insecurity and malnutrition; the elimination of poverty and the driving forward of economic and social progress for all; and, the sustainable management and utilization of natural resources, including land, water, air, climate and genetic resources for the benefit of present and future generations. For more information, click here to view the FAO website.

United Nations University - Institute for the Advanced Study of Sustainability

The United Nations University - Institute for the Advanced Study of Sustainability (UNU-IAS) is a leading research and teaching institute based in Tokyo, Japan. Its mission is to advance efforts towards a more sustainable future, through policy-oriented research and capacity development focused on sustainability and its social, economic and environmental dimensions. For more information, click here to view the UNU-IAS website.

World Resources Forum

The World Resources Forum is a science-based platform created to exchange knowledge about the economic, political and environmental implications of global resource use. In partnership with the CBD, workshops focusing on Urban Eco-Efficiency began at COP 11in Hyderabad, India as “a first step towards the establishment of a longer-term cooperative work programme to promote sustainable consumption and production (SC) at sub-national and local levels” to promote the implementation of the Strategic Plan for Biodiversity 2011-2020. For more information, click here to visit the WRF website.
